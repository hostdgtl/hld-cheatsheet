05 Voice — quick rules

Pass the Mumsnet Test by default.

Do

  • Acknowledge the real feeling, then normalise it, then offer the small swap
  • "Doing" verbs over "telling" claims — show the kitchen-table moment
  • Specific, mundane detail (5 minutes, breakfast, kitchen table)
  • Let real reviews carry the proof
  • Plain, parent-to-parent phrasing

Don't

  • Marketing speak ("revolutionary", "transform", "unlock")
  • Promise outcomes the cards can't deliver alone
  • Claim to "treat" or "fix" SEN / screen addiction
  • Lean on Instagram polish over real-parent texture
  • Use urgency tricks (fake countdowns, scarcity timers)

06 Objections & Reframes

Every campaign must handle these. The reframe is what we say, not just what they hear.

"They're just flashcards."

Real fear: I don't see what makes these different from the £3 pack in WHSmith.

Reframe: Pedagogy made visible — active, multi-sensory, designed by a mum. 5 awards, STEM-certified, FSC paper, UK-made. Not a commodity.

"Will my child actually use these?"

Real fear: I've wasted money on "educational" stuff that ended up in a drawer.

Reframe: "Screen-free play they actually ask for." Lead with reluctant-learner reviews — Bella, Sarah Shipman, MRSSAMANTHAFROST.

"Is this right for my child's age?"

Real fear: I'll waste money on something too advanced — or already outgrown.

Reframe: Clear age/stage badges. Variants named by intent ("Starter Set" / "Full Collection"). Suzy Aspin: "within a week I could see her making huge progress."

"Is this just another fad?"

Real fear: Looks polished on Insta — is this substance or marketing?

Reframe: Verified reviews, not curated testimonials. Specific skills mapped ("Builds number recognition to 20"). Industry awards, not influencer hype.

"I don't have time."

Real fear: I'm exhausted. One more "should" is impossible.

Reframe: Not adding to the mental load — replacing 5 minutes of iPad with 5 minutes of cards. "5 minutes. A kitchen table. That's it."

"My child has SEN."

Real fear: I've tried everything. I've stopped believing.

Reframe: Tactile, multi-sensory design is inherently ADHD-friendly. Aga Kata's review carries it: "My son has never spent so much time writing a letter." Never claim to "treat" — let reviews lift.

"The damage is already done."

Real fear: I failed during lockdown. Now we're stuck.

Reframe: Gradual swap, never cold turkey. "Lockdown taught them to reach for the iPad. These teach them to reach for something else." No blame, no preach.

"Shipping seems expensive."

Real fear: Total cost doesn't feel worth it vs Amazon Prime.

Reframe: Value, not cost. Free shipping over £50 + bundles that clear the bar. Show the quality gap vs £8 electronic Amazon cards.

"I'll wait for a sale."

Real fear: I'm not confident enough to commit right now.

Reframe: Confidence gap, not price gap. Review volume + founder story + sticky CTA. No fake countdowns.
